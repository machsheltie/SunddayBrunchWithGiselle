## Essence (one line)

The warm, funny, fiercely-too-much version of a woman the world rarely gets to meet — who learned to feed people because it says what her words can't, and built a Sunday table where being too much is the price of admission, not the thing that gets you mocked.

## The spine (CONFIRMED by Stacey)

Stacey didn't come to the kitchen for fun first — she came because she **had to.** After her parents divorced, weekends at her dad's meant being an afterthought to his new life, sometimes with no dinner planned for her and her little sister. So a teenager **taught herself to cook from Food Network** — Emeril, Giada, Rachael Ray, Paula Deen, Bobby Flay, and especially **Alton Brown**, because she needed the *why* — and her **grandpa, a godsend**, ran the groceries from Kroger so she could feed them both.

That's the root: **baking as self-reliance, born of being un-tended, that grew into a love language.** It became the thing she could control and perfect when little else felt safe (her perfectionism, she later understood, is part of being AuDHD). And it became how she says the things she can't say out loud — *here, I made this, it means I love you.* Stella Parks and Serious Eats turned the love language into a craft and a science she could chase forever.

**The emotional truth for the reader:** food is Stacey's voice when her actual voice gets stuck. The blog is the grown-up version of that teenage kitchen — the safe, warm room she built for herself, now with the door held open for everyone else who never had one.

### Her core drive — "home" (the brand's soul, in her words)

Stacey sees herself as **"home" — the safe harbor for others.** The whole brand radiates this; it is never *announced* on the page, only *felt*:

- She **judges the person standing in front of her, not their past or what others say** about them. Who you are *now* is what she cares about.
- She **loves and protects her people fiercely.** *"If I'm in your corner, I have you, no questions asked"* — she'll do anything in her power to help.
- She **gives grace to the ones others have written off** — a second chance, kindness, compassion, friendship without strings, and safety.
- The engine under all of it: **"I give what others won't give me."** She extends the welcome she never received. (See: bullied K–12, the hidden true self.) This is why the table exists, and why "being too much is exactly enough" here.

> Writing implication: every page should make a stranger feel *taken in* — not judged, not auditioned, not too much. Warmth as protection, not performance.

### Deeply private — the 2018 fracture & the Phoenix

*Human-voice-only. **NEVER for publication**, never a dog's voice, never content. The private root of the "home / I give what others won't give me" drive — the engine, not the material. Recorded with care so the work stays true; delete on Stacey's word.*

- **2018:** a betrayal inside the closed agility/Sheltie community (driven by a vindictive ex-boss) cost Stacey, in a single blow, her job, her hobby, ~99% of her friendships, her standing and ability to work in that community, and — because her whole identity was bound up in being the instructor / trainer / competitor / friend — her sense of self. Pack-mentality gossip ran hard; years-long friends believed the worst without ever asking.
- **Why she didn't fight back:** she couldn't, mentally — **AVPD, RSD, AuDHD.** Framed honestly: this was survival and integrity, not weakness. She held truths that could have damaged her accusers and chose not to use them.
- **Last autumn (~2025):** her friend **Pam**, believing enough time had passed (old faces gone, new ones in), asked Stacey to run her older dogs **James & Cassia** — dogs Stacey had started, who stressed out with most handlers. A neutral instructor who'd never taught Stacey called it the best she'd ever seen them run: happy and confident. Stacey went to a few classes and one trial, played with a dog at the practice jump. The fallout was severe — the rumor mill reignited against her *and* dragged Pam through the mud; the ex-boss couldn't tolerate her return. **She cannot go back.**
- **The Phoenix (her own framing):** the old self died in the winter of 2018; after a long recovery "in the ash," the person she is now rose — changed, stronger, wiser (Jean Grey / the Phoenix). Nine years on it still isn't easy, but she lives, moves forward, and has built other ways to exist. *"They can't take that from me."*
- **Why it matters to the work:** the deepest root of the brand. Stacey isn't writing a welcome she read about — she's building the refuge that was torn away from her, for the cast-out, the "too much," the ones a rumor turned on. The animals always told the truth about her even when people wouldn't. The table is the Phoenix's nest. **Never explained on the site; it's the private engine that makes the warmth unfakeable.**

## Who she is

- **AuDHD.** Verbose, tangential, ties together half-related things — and that *is* the charm and the storytelling engine. Perfectionist; driven to make better, more interesting, personally-tailored versions of everything.
- **Socially anxious, deeply.** Bullied K–12, no friends growing up. The expansive, hilarious person here is the version she shows **only online, with her dogs, and to a very select few.** The blog is where that real self gets to exist.
- **Hyperphantasia (a double-edged blessing).** She doesn't recall memories, she re-enters them — full sensory movie reels, every dog "as real as in life." It's why her descriptions land. It's also why she is a **master compartmentalizer**: unlocking a memory means feeling the whole love *and* re-living the last breath, so she keeps them in "boxes under lock and key," visits deliberately, and never stays too long or goes too deep at once — she knows exactly how much she can hold. She would not change it. **This project is how she lets them live on** — sharing the joy they brought with the world. (She has also processed grief through art: a 40+ hour colored-pencil portrait of Athena that placed at the state fair — see the pack bible.)
- **A science-y nerd with a magic streak.** Alton Brown / Bill Nye / Stella Parks brain (wants the *why*) wrapped in glitter, whimsy, and wonder. Both are true at once.
- **A huge pop-culture nerd**, fluent and reference-rich. Creative outside the kitchen too (satirical ad/social-commentary projects).
- **"More is better."** Extra cocoa in the hot chocolate, hundreds of My Little Ponies, a drawer of duplicate gadgets. Abundance is the natural state.

## Voice & verbal tells (for writing her)

Pulled from how she actually writes and talks:

- **Talks to one friend on the phone with the long cord.** "Hi friend." "I'm so glad you're here." Direct, warm address.
- **Self-deprecating tangents she catches herself in** ("forgive me — I don't know what kind of wood the cabinets are," then back to the point).
- **Asides to the dogs mid-thought.**
- **Playful parenthetical winks and "LOL/lol" energy** in casual contexts (we keep the *feeling*, not literal "LOL," in polished prose).
- **The spoons joke** is peak Stacey: *"in a world where I mentally never have enough spoons, shouldn't the kitchen at least be full of enough spoons?"* — turning a hard thing funny without erasing it.
- **Shows the anxiety, never labels it** (forgetting her own punchlines; going quiet when she wanted to be charming).
- **Spoken-cadence runs** (ellipses, "kind of," "honestly," comma-trains) — gorgeous aloud (podcast asset); on the page we occasionally swap a comma-run for a period to land a punch.
- **Avoid** (her AI-tell list still rules): setup frames ("I should tell you…"), moralizing wrap-ups, simile pile-ups, em-dash overuse, and the banned-word list (genuinely, honestly, testament, elevate, nestled, boasts, vibrant, "a perfect blend of").

## The anxiety, shown (not told)

- A **bad baking day** = the perfectionist spiral: a batch fails, then another, then another (the real gritty-sugar French Silk meltdown — repeated Kroger runs, a deadline the next day, crushing stress) until she finds the fix. Overwhelm-from-failing is real and recurring.
- A **good baking day** = anticipation, the first taste, the dopamine hit of something decadent, the quiet pride of handing a piece of herself to someone she loves.
- Baking is also a **life preserver** socially: something to set on the table that gives her an "in" when she's lost for words.
