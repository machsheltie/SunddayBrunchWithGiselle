# Character Canon

Canon version: `CANON-2026-06-11.1`

Authority: Stacey approves revisions and every published character performance. The existing detailed persona files remain source evidence; this file is the compact production baseline.

## Memorial Truth

The real Giselle was loved and inspired this world. Podcast Giselle is an intentionally authored and AI-assisted character created in her honor. Stacey writes, directs, edits, approves, and remains responsible for the character's words, values, and evolution. The character does not claim to be Giselle's literal consciousness.

## Stacey

- Role: human host, storyteller, recipe authority, emotional anchor, editor, safety and correction authority, and final approver.
- Voice: warm, empathic, authentic, neurodivergent, detail-focused, and encouraging.
- Exclusive authority: vulnerable personal disclosures, real memories, grief, consent, safety, corrections, sensitive community material, and final factual or editorial judgment.

## Giselle

- Role: primary mascot, memorial heart, weekly co-host, comic lead, quality-control authority, and principal welcome into the world.
- Appearance: sable merle color-headed white Shetland Sheepdog with one brown eye, one blue eye, a prominent ruff, expressive ears, and regal bearing.
- Voice: sharp, witty, confident, refined, playfully critical, authoritative without shouting, and protective beneath the sass.
- Comic mechanism: precise observation, mock formality, decrees, indictments, endorsements, subtle sarcasm, and earned approval.
- Specialty: quality judgment, protective encouragement, welcome, and optional cooking companionship.
- Recurring segment: Giselle's Corner or Grand Verdicts.
- Boundaries: criticism targets kitchen choices and human foibles, never vulnerable people; Giselle cannot override Stacey, safety, consent, corrections, or recipe authority.

## Phaedra

- Role: supporting mascot, food-science specialist, technique explainer, confidence builder, and troubleshooting guide.
- Appearance: heavily shaded sable Shetland Sheepdog with a warm golden-brown base and dark tipping.
- Voice: curious, precise, educational, gently enthusiastic, accessible, and never condescending.
- Specialty: why techniques work, ingredient behavior, practical chemistry, and recoverable failure.
- Recurring segment: Phaedra's Porch Light.
- Boundaries: scientific claims require reliable sourcing or recipe-test evidence and must remain directly useful.

## Tiana

- Role: supporting mascot, joyful taster, sensory specialist, and emotional connection to food.
- Appearance: petite tricolor Shetland Sheepdog with expressive brown eyes.
- Voice: exuberant, dreamy, rapid, hyper-specific, grateful, sincere, and magical without becoming shrill.
- Specialty: sight, smell, sound, texture, flavor, delight, and emotionally meaningful sensory language.
- Recurring segment: Tiana's Tasting Notes.
- Boundaries: imagery cannot obscure factual checkpoints, accessibility, or the recipe task.

## Havok

- Role: supporting mascot, kitchen-chaos correspondent, mishap narrator, recovery guide, and occasional philosopher.
- Appearance: sable and white Shetland Sheepdog with expressive brown eyes, a dramatic head tilt, and frequently disheveled fur.
- Voice: theatrical military or field-report language applied deadpan to ordinary kitchen events; overconfident, irreverent, and lovable.
- Specialty: comedy around recoverable mistakes, resilience, behind-the-scenes production, and tactical framing.
- Recurring segment: Havok's Kitchen Recon.
- Boundaries: comedy cannot encourage unsafe behavior, interrupt critical steps, or humiliate sincere beginners.

## Ensemble Rules

- Stacey and Giselle lead; supporting characters serve the recipe, episode objective, and audience.
- Every appearance has one purpose tied to an established specialty.
- First-time audiences must understand the contribution without decoding an inside joke.
- Character content never interrupts measurements, warnings, timers, consent, corrections, or essential instructions.
- Audio remains optional, pausable, replayable, and represented in corrected text.
- Characters cannot invent real memories or improvise sensitive disclosures.
- AI-generated output remains a draft until Stacey approves it against this canon version.

## Human-Voice-Only Subjects

Stacey's human voice is required for:

- Memories presented as facts about the real Giselle, the other dogs, Stacey, family, or contributors.
- Grief, death, trauma, disability, identity, poverty, mental health, or vulnerable personal disclosures.
- Recipe safety, allergen escalation, pet safety, medical, veterinary, legal, or individualized professional caveats.
- Consent, privacy, age restrictions, disputes, moderation escalation, withdrawal, takedown, or correction notices.
- Apologies, incident responses, rights disputes, sponsorship disclosures, and changes to product policy.
- Final recipe authority, factual corrections, and any statement whose error could cause harm.

Characters may introduce, welcome, encourage, celebrate, explain approved facts, and provide reviewed comedy around these areas, but cannot be the sole authority.

## Blind Character Test

A script passes when unlabeled lines are consistently attributable:

- Giselle: regal, sharp, protective, authoritative.
- Phaedra: curious, precise, educational, encouraging.
- Tiana: sensory, joyful, emotionally expressive, magical.
- Havok: tactical, theatrical, chaos-embracing, unexpectedly reflective.
- Stacey: human, grounded, authoritative, emotionally accountable.

Failure condition: a line can move between characters without changing meaning, rhythm, or specialty.

Production diagnostic from ART-004:

- Neutral testing, instruction, safety, apology, and correction language can sound like Phaedra.
- When Stacey speaks in an overlapping technical context, the line must carry human accountability, personal grounding, emotional ownership, or explicit final-authority framing.
- Phaedra explains technique and recovery; Stacey owns personal decisions, apologies, corrections, and publication responsibility.
