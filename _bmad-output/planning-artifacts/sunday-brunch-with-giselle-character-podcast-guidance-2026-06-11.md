# Sophia's Character And Podcast Guidance

## Purpose

This document preserves and reconciles Sophia's character, narrative, podcast, community, and merchandise guidance from the two BMAD Party Mode rounds held on 2026-06-11.

It is a strategic companion to the detailed persona files. It does not replace the existing character biographies, voice banks, sample lines, segment concepts, or Episode 1 scripts. Its purpose is to explain how those established characters should function together across the podcast, website, memorial story, community, and future merchandise.

## Settled Correction

Sophia's first-round phrase that Giselle should remain "a presence rather than becoming a mascot" has been superseded by the creator's settled decision:

- Giselle is intentionally the primary mascot.
- Giselle is a performed character and the weekly AI-voiced co-host.
- Phaedra, Tiana, and Havok are supporting mascots.
- The character and merchandise strategy is intentional, central, and distinctive.

The useful principle inside Sophia's original caution remains:

> Mascot status must not flatten Giselle into a hollow logo, generic spokesperson, or sales device.

Giselle may be mascot, co-host, comic lead, emotional center, and commercial character. The protection comes from honest authorship, disciplined canon, humane boundaries, and the creator's unmistakable hand on every story.

# Narrative Foundation

## What The Memorial Means

The heart of Sunday Brunch With Giselle is not only that Giselle died. It is that she lived, was loved, and changed the atmosphere of a home.

Grief is part of the foundation, but it should not become the only experience offered on every page or episode. Visitors did not need to know Giselle personally. They need to understand what she represents:

- Companionship
- Welcome
- Mischief
- Comfort
- Loyalty
- Humor
- The small rituals through which love continues

The project should introduce Giselle first as funny, capable, affectionate, opinionated, and alive within the story world. The memorial origin can then deepen the relationship without making death the price of admission.

## Central Editorial Promise

> Come as you are. Make something with care. Share the table with those you love.

## Three Truths That Keep The AI Co-Host Honest

The audience should be able to understand:

1. The real Giselle was loved and inspired this world.
2. Podcast Giselle is an intentionally created and performed character inspired by that love.
3. Stacey is the human author, director, editor, and person responsible for Giselle's words, values, and evolution.

The character does not need to claim to be Giselle's literal consciousness. She can carry wit, habits, warmth, protectiveness, comedy, and emotional legacy into a continuing creative work.

Comedy does not diminish the memorial. Carefully handled laughter demonstrates that the relationship still creates life.

## Blind Character Test

Before weekly production begins, dialogue should be distinguishable without speaker labels:

- Giselle sounds unmistakably regal, sharp, protective, and authoritative.
- Phaedra sounds curious, precise, educational, and warmly encouraging.
- Tiana sounds sensory, joyful, emotionally expressive, and sincerely magical.
- Havok sounds theatrically tactical, chaos-embracing, overconfident, and unexpectedly reflective.
- Stacey remains the human center, storyteller, recipe authority, and emotional anchor.

If lines can be exchanged among characters without changing their meaning or rhythm, the script needs revision.

## Ensemble Rule

The ensemble supports the recipe and the human host. It should not overwhelm either.

Character appearances should:

- Have a purpose
- Match an established specialty
- Reward returning listeners
- Remain understandable to first-time visitors
- Never interrupt a measurement, warning, timer, or essential instruction
- Never require the audience to decode an inside joke before using the recipe

# Recipe Story Guidance

Every recipe should follow a dependable editorial rhythm:

1. A brief opening scene or reason the recipe matters
2. A clear statement of what the visitor will make
3. Time, yield, difficulty, equipment, and dietary information
4. Tested ingredients and instructions
5. Sensory evidence of progress
6. Likely mistakes and ways to recover
7. Storage, substitutions, make-ahead, and reheating guidance
8. A short invitation to share the result

Personal stories should enrich the recipe without delaying the cooking task. Visitors should have a visible route to the recipe, while the full story remains available to those who came for companionship.

## Character Use On Recipe Pages

- Giselle may welcome, judge, endorse, or provide protective encouragement.
- Phaedra should explain why a technique works.
- Tiana should explain what the baker should notice through sight, smell, taste, sound, or texture.
- Havok may frame a common mishap or recovery as a tactical report.
- Character contributions should be clearly separated from authoritative measurements and safety instructions.
- Longer emotional stories belong in companion articles or podcast material when they would crowd the functional recipe.

## Recipe To Podcast Relationships

Mark whether a recipe is:

- Featured in an episode
- Inspired by a character
- Inspired by a listener
- Suitable for an audio bake-along
- Supported by troubleshooting clips
- Part of a Sunday menu

# First Season Guidance

Sophia proposed two useful eight-part arcs. They can be treated as two options or combined.

## Arc A: Setting The Table Again

This arc moves from silence back toward participation. It does not imply that grief is completed or overcome.

1. Why the table exists
2. The first recipe resumed after the pause
3. Comfort food for difficult mornings
4. Baking with four attentive Shelties nearby
5. What companions teach us about presence
6. Inviting another person or pet into the story
7. A community-requested recipe
8. The first full Sunday table

## Arc B: Introducing The Ensemble

This arc teaches listeners how the comic and culinary world works.

1. Welcome to Sunday Brunch
2. The Biscuit Incident
3. Everyone Has an Opinion About Pancakes
4. The Recipe That Refused to Behave
5. Four Shelties, One Kitchen
6. A Recipe Requested With Love
7. Setting the Table Again
8. There Is Always Room for One More

## Recommended Reconciliation

Use Arc B as the public-facing dramatic structure and weave Arc A's emotional progression through it.

Important sequencing principle:

- Do not make the memorial episode the premiere.
- First let listeners know Giselle as funny, capable, affectionate, and alive within the story world.
- Reveal the memorial foundation after the audience understands the relationship and tone.

The memorial should deepen the experience, not serve as the admission price.

# Voice Stewardship

AI performance requires continuing human control.

## Required Rules

- Stacey approves every final script and published line.
- AI output never becomes canon automatically.
- Characters do not invent memories of the real Giselle or other dogs.
- Emotional disclosures about Stacey require explicit human authorship.
- Sensitive community stories are not improvised by characters.
- Unsafe or inaccurate cooking guidance is corrected and documented.
- The ensemble does not obscure Stacey's authority as host and recipe guide.
- Generated source audio and mastered releases are archived separately.
- Every performance can be traced to a script and canon version.

## Comedy Boundaries

Comedy may target:

- Vanity
- Kitchen chaos
- Rivalry
- Character flaws
- Overconfidence
- Familiar baking mistakes
- The absurdity of domestic life

Comedy should not target:

- Grief
- Disability
- Poverty
- Identity
- Trauma
- A vulnerable contributor
- A person's appearance
- A sincere beginner's failure

Humor may coexist with sorrow, but it should never mock the person who is hurting.

## Performance Disclosure

Use clear, warm language explaining that:

- The Shelties are authored characters.
- Their performances are AI-assisted.
- Stacey writes, directs, edits, and approves them.
- The character Giselle is inspired by the real Giselle and created in her honor.

The disclosure should be accessible and honest without appearing before every joke or interrupting the story world.

# Editorial Boundaries

- Giselle's death is never used as a conversion tactic.
- Newsletter prompts do not sit directly beside vulnerable grief passages.
- Products are not described as proof of love or memory.
- Fictional dialogue is understood as part of the show's authored story world.
- Private memories remain private unless sharing serves a deliberate purpose.
- Community grief stories require explicit permission, sensitive editing, and withdrawal rights.
- Stacey handles vulnerable disclosures, memorials, consent, and safety.
- The Shelties may welcome, tease gently, encourage, and celebrate.

# Sustainable Publishing Guidance

Sophia's first-round cadence proposal was:

- One flagship recipe each week
- One supporting article, technique guide, or pet-safe kitchen note
- One podcast episode every two weeks
- One monthly Sunday menu
- Community features only after moderation is ready

The creator has since settled on a weekly podcast. The updated operating plan is:

- One weekly podcast episode
- A connected flagship recipe or meaningful companion item
- Supporting articles when capacity permits
- One monthly Sunday menu connecting recipes, story, and audio
- A completed episode buffer before public launch
- Community features only after moderation and consent systems are operational

The weekly promise should begin only after four consecutive private production cycles and at least two completed reserve episodes.

# Merchandise As Story

Merchandise should feel like an artifact from the world, not a logo placed on generic inventory.

## Start With Proven Audience Language

- Frequently repeated sayings
- Listener-favorite exchanges
- Recipe-specific jokes
- Sticker expressions tied to each specialty
- Seasonal Sunday Brunch illustrations
- Recognizable podcast moments

## Recommended Product Sequence

1. Stickers
2. Small paper goods
3. Recipe cards, prints, or journals
4. Limited shirts
5. Kitchen textiles and mugs
6. Seasonal or story-driven collections

## Commercial Boundaries

- Memorial grief never pressures a purchase.
- Never claim that Giselle would want someone to buy an item.
- The real dog's death is not sentimental sales copy.
- Products celebrate character, companionship, humor, shared sayings, and the world created in her honor.
- Community pets do not appear on merchandise without separate commercial permission.
- Charitable claims must be specific, verifiable, and transparent.
- Merchandise does not interrupt recipe instructions or vulnerable memorial writing.

## Merchandise Gate

Begin only after:

- Character recognition is sustained.
- Audiences repeatedly quote or request specific material.
- Trademark and rights review is complete.
- Visual standards are stable.
- Product quality is tested.
- Fulfillment will not disrupt recipes or podcast production.

# Website Placement Guidance

## Homepage

- Stacey and Giselle lead the weekly feature.
- Establish that the Shelties are a cast early enough to set expectations.
- Supporting characters preview their specialties without competing with the primary action.
- The principal action remains clear: find the recipe, hear the episode, or join the Sunday letter.

## Recipe Discovery

Allow browsing by:

- Dish
- Occasion
- Skill
- Dietary need
- Ingredient
- Season
- Episode
- Character specialty

Character specialty is an additional discovery path, not a replacement for standard labels.

## Recipe Pages

- Preserve the functional recipe hierarchy.
- Give character modules consistent, recognizable locations.
- Keep audio optional.
- Provide text equivalents.
- Link to related episodes without forcing playback.

## Cook Mode

- Giselle may open and close the experience.
- Character audio appears only at intentional points.
- Essential instructions remain independently readable.
- Audio has pause, replay, volume, and text equivalents.
- Character lines do not obscure timers, warnings, or measurements.

## Podcast Pages

- Include visual cast information.
- Show related recipes and exact recipe links.
- Include transcripts, chapters, memorable sayings, and character credits.
- Make the AI-assisted performance context available.

## Community

- Use bounded character-hosted categories.
- Keep responses curated and reviewed.
- Avoid uncontrolled personalized advice.

## Commerce

- Integrate products as curated artifacts from established stories and sayings.
- Keep the store visually connected but functionally separate from recipes and memorial content.

# Character And Episode Review Checklist

Before approving a script or character module:

- [ ] The speaker is unmistakable without a label.
- [ ] The line matches the current canon version.
- [ ] The character contributes through an established specialty.
- [ ] The line advances cooking, story, comedy, or emotional truth.
- [ ] The joke remains understandable to a first-time listener.
- [ ] The character does not invent real memories.
- [ ] Safety and measurements remain under human authority.
- [ ] Vulnerable material remains in Stacey's voice.
- [ ] The line does not mock a vulnerable person.
- [ ] The ensemble does not overwhelm the episode's objective.
- [ ] AI-generated output has received human approval.
- [ ] Transcript and accessibility equivalents are prepared.
- [ ] Reusable sayings are logged for continuity.
- [ ] Any potential merchandise use remains separate from vulnerable memorial material.

# Phase Gates For The Podcast And Character World

## Gate 1: Canon Ready

- Characters pass blind distinction.
- Boundaries and specialties are documented.
- AI disclosure is approved.
- Voice and asset records exist.

## Gate 2: Production Ready

- Episode template is stable.
- Four private weekly cycles succeed.
- Two completed episodes remain in reserve.
- Scripts, audio, transcripts, chapters, and archives follow one workflow.

## Gate 3: Audience Ready

- Listeners understand the host-character relationship.
- Recipes and episodes strengthen each other.
- Accessibility and transcripts meet the quality standard.
- Character recognition is measurable without confusing fiction and memorial truth.

## Gate 4: Community Ready

- Moderation, consent, privacy, withdrawal, and release processes operate.
- Stacey retains control over sensitive material.

## Gate 5: Commerce Ready

- Audience demand is real.
- Rights and visual standards are settled.
- Products celebrate the world without exploiting grief.
- Fulfillment will not damage the weekly show or recipe quality.

# Final Character Principle

Sunday Brunch With Giselle should make visitors feel that someone left a place for them at the table.

The recipes must earn trust. The stories must earn attention. The characters must earn affection. Giselle's memorial foundation must remain honest, but her role is not confined to sorrow. She may remain funny, regal, protective, commercial, magical, and vividly present because the creator is deliberately carrying love forward through art.
