*A cold, cloud-soft chocolate pie that tastes like a fancy restaurant dessert and is secretly mostly patience. This is our first recipe — the one that started everything.*

**Active time:** about 30 minutes  ·  **Chill:** at least 2 hours  ·  **Total:** about 2½ hours  ·  **Makes:** one 9-inch pie (8 slices)  ·  **Difficulty:** easier than it looks

---

## Read this first

Three things decide whether this pie turns out, so I'm putting them right up top instead of burying them.

1. **Use pasteurized eggs — or cook the eggs (easy backup below).** The filling isn't baked, so the eggs go in raw. Pasteurized eggs are heat-treated to be safe to eat uncooked; look for "pasteurized" printed on the carton in the regular egg case. Can't find them? No problem at all — there's a quick cooked-egg method in the Notes that works just as well.
2. **A stand mixer earns its keep here.** You'll beat the filling for about 20 minutes total. A hand mixer works too; just settle in and be ready to switch arms.
3. **Don't rush the beating.** That's the whole secret to "silk." When your arm gets bored, keep going — I'll tell you exactly what to watch for.

**Contains:** wheat/gluten, milk, and eggs, plus soy (soy lecithin is in most chocolate — check your labels). Depending on the chocolate brand, it may also carry traces of tree nuts or peanuts, so read the package if that matters at your table.

**A note for the pups:** this one stays on the human side of the table. Chocolate is toxic to dogs, and the dark, unsweetened kind in this filling is the most dangerous of all, so keep every slice well out of reach of your own Giselles. (Ours supervise strictly from an advisory distance.)

## Equipment

- 9-inch deep glass pie dish
- Stand mixer (or a hand mixer and a little stubbornness)
- Small microwave-safe bowl
- Parchment paper
- Instant-read thermometer (only if you're using the cooked-egg method)

---

## Ingredients

**For the chocolate filling**

- ½ box refrigerated pie crust (one crust)
- 4 oz unsweetened baking chocolate, 100% cacao (about 113 g)
- 1 cup (226 g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
- 1½ cups (300 g) granulated sugar
- 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
- 4 large eggs, pasteurized *(see Read This First)*

**For the whipped cream topping**

- 1 cup (240 ml) heavy whipping cream, cold
- ⅔ cup (about 80 g) powdered sugar

**For the chocolate shards on top**

- 1 cup (about 170 g) milk chocolate chips

## Instructions

**1. Bake and fully cool the crust.**
Bake the single crust empty, following the package directions (usually: prick it all over, then bake until golden). Now let it cool completely. A warm crust melts the filling, so don't rush — go start step 2 while it cools.

**2. Melt the chocolate.**
Put the unsweetened chocolate in a small microwave-safe bowl. Microwave on high for 1 minute, stir, then keep going in 15-second bursts, stirring each time, until smooth. Set it aside to cool to room temperature. (Warm chocolate melts the butter and you'll end up with soup instead of fluff.)

**3. Beat the butter and sugar.**
In the stand mixer, beat the softened butter with the granulated sugar on medium-high until pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes. You're looking for a noticeably lighter color.

**4. Add the chocolate and vanilla.**
With the mixer on medium, slowly drizzle in the cooled chocolate, then beat in the vanilla. Scrape down the bowl so it's all evenly mixed.

**5. Add the eggs, one at a time — this is the important part.**
Add one egg and beat a full 5 minutes before you add the next. Repeat with all four eggs, beating 5 minutes after each. Yes, that's about 20 minutes total, and yes, it feels like forever. Set a timer, put on a podcast (ahem), and let the machine do the work.
*What you're watching for:* the filling goes from grainy and loose to thick, glossy, and silky-smooth. Rub a little between two fingers — if you still feel sugar grains, it needs more time.

**6. Fill the crust.**
Scrape the filling into your cooled crust and smooth the top.

**7. Whip the cream and chill the pie.**
Beat the cold cream on high until it holds stiff peaks, about 3 minutes. Add the powdered sugar and beat just until smooth and fluffy. Spread it over the pie. Refrigerate at least 2 hours, or overnight — the filling needs that chill to set firm enough to slice cleanly.

**8. Make the chocolate shards while the pie chills.**
Melt the milk chocolate chips the same way as before: 1 minute, then 15-second bursts, stirring, until smooth. Spread it very, *very* thinly over a large sheet of parchment. Roll the parchment into a tube (like a paper-towel roll) and refrigerate at least 30 minutes. Unroll it and the chocolate cracks into pretty shards. Scatter them over the pie just before serving.

Slice it cold, serve, and accept the compliments. Enjoy!

## Notes & troubleshooting

**No pasteurized eggs? Cook them instead.** In a saucepan over low-medium heat, whisk the 4 eggs with the 1½ cups sugar constantly until a thermometer reads 160°F (this gently cooks the eggs so they're safe). Take it off the heat and stir in the melted, cooled chocolate, then beat about 5 minutes. Separately, beat the softened butter until fluffy, about 3 minutes, then beat in the chocolate-egg mixture and the vanilla. Spread into your cooled crust and jump to step 7 (whipped cream).

> **🐾 Phaedra's Porch Light** — *why it works, and how to fix it if it doesn't*
>
> Here's something wonderful: that long beat is doing two invisible jobs at once. It slowly dissolves the sugar so the filling turns satin-smooth instead of gritty, and it whisks in thousands of tiny air bubbles that give the pie its light, mousse-like body. That's the entire difference between "fine" and "silk."
>
> And if something looks off, there's almost always a temperature reason and a gentle fix:
>
> - *Soupy or loose filling?* Your chocolate or butter was still warm. Everything wants to be at room temperature, never hot. Chill the bowl 15 minutes and re-whip.
> - *Grainy or gritty?* The sugar hasn't finished dissolving. Keep beating — you really can't overdo this step.
> - *Whipped cream won't hold peaks?* The cream or the bowl is too warm. Cold cream in a cold bowl whips up beautifully.
> - *Crust melting at the edges?* It went in before it was fully cool. A little patience here pays off every time.
>
> Understand the why, and you can rescue almost anything. You've got this.

**Make ahead:** The whole pie can be made a day in advance and kept covered in the fridge. Add the chocolate shards right before serving so they stay crisp.

**Storing:** Keep leftovers loosely covered in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It's a cold pie through and through, so it doesn't love sitting out at room temperature.
