## The reframe (read this first)

Stacey is real — she IS the author. The four Shelties are her four real dogs, anthropomorphized into talking, emoting versions that capture their true essences. The blog/podcast characters are "what they would be if they were talking little Shelties instead of dogs who can't speak."

**Giselle passed away unexpectedly in late February 2026, at 11 years old** (bladder ruptured; made it through surgery, passed during the night). Stacey tabled the project in fresh grief, then decided to continue it **as a tribute to her** — so Giselle, and the others, live on through these stories. Framing in Stacey's words: dogs are shooting stars — they come into our lives, make them better, and one day their star burns out and their souls go to the rainbow bridge to wait for us. The price of love, happily paid. (Cf. Garth Brooks, "The Dance.")

> **This means the project is a love letter/tribute first, a recipe blog second.** Grief + whimsy are not in tension here; they're the same warmth. This is the brand's real, un-fakeable differentiator.

**Tender boundary:** Giselle's passing, grief, and all real memories of the real dogs are **human-voice-only (Stacey)** per the existing guides. The dogs never narrate a real memory. Any acknowledgment of Giselle's death is Stacey's voice alone, handled with care — never a dog's.

## Stacey (the human; narrator; real)

The author. Warm, socially anxious, neurodivergent, nostalgic; a compassionate overthinker. Works from home; the dogs are her constant companions. Has a long history with these dogs: present at each one's birth, the first hand to ever touch them, and the last presence they'll know in life. Competed in agility for years, traveling the country; now does nosework, hiking, and agility at home. A friend once said her Shelties act as if "the world rises and sets on her" — and in their little worlds, it does.

Has a neurodivergent partner (SO) who does coregulation "bids" — in a childlike, vulnerable voice asks "rub me" (rub his arm/back/belly) when he needs co-regulation. (Relevant because Phaedra does the dog version of this — see Phaedra.)

Brand origin / thesis (Stacey's): conceived last summer, summed up as **"Lamb Chop for grown-ups."** For the 80s/90s kids who grew up with Lamb Chop, Mr. Rogers, Captain Kangaroo, and Sharon, Lois & Bram's Elephant Show — who may be neurodivergent, are looking for a little magic, warmth, and whimsy, are pet lovers, and need a great baking recipe.

Self-assessment: "I'm probably the least fleshed-out character — probably because it IS me. I've been so focused on creating the characters to match the Shelties; I just kind of *am*."

## The Pack — real essences

### Giselle — the Queen (sable merle, 11)
Sable merle Shetland Sheepdog. Didn't care for many people; could be judgmental of them. Loved Stacey *intensely* — once, playing in the field with Stacey's ex, she spotted Stacey across the field, let out a loud joyful shriek of a bark, and ran full-tilt up to the deck to get to her. Opinionated, full of personality. 14 lbs.

Signature behaviors:
- **"Ramping and rowling":** decides she wants attention, comes over wagging and staring, starts smacking Stacey HARD with her feet, digging at the ground with one foot, grumbling/growling low — ramps up harder if she's not getting her way, into a weird drawn-out growly sound, then full barking.
- **Brilliant and unrepentant:** in a crate after grocery shopping, stuck her foot through the bars, pawed open a box of strawberries, pulled them in one at a time, and ate them all before they got home. NOT sorry.
- **Demanded to be picked up:** paced and clicked her nails on the floor until Stacey woke/noticed; once lifted into bed she'd beat Stacey back to her spot, undercut her, claim the space (and a pillow or two), and body-slam in as hard as 14 lbs allowed. Then cuddle, wanting pets and sweet talk. Always wanted to be with Stacey.

Persona role (existing): **Giselle's Grand Verdict** — the queen, quality control, Devil-Wears-Prada-meets-Queen-of-Hearts, sharp/mock-formal with deep protectiveness underneath. Opens the story, closes the verdict.

`<AI>` Her real "ramping and rowling" — escalating demands until she's obeyed — IS the Queen. The persona is already true to life. The body-slam-into-your-spot and the unrepentant strawberry heist are pure "I have standards / just like me" energy. Her one real soft tell (loving Stacey so much she shrieks with joy) is the protectiveness-under-the-sass the guide already calls for. `</AI>`

### Phaedra — the Dogter (shaded sable, 8)
Shaded sable Shetland Sheepdog. **Only 5 days apart in age from Tiana; they've been together their whole lives and are genuine best friends** — always together, sleep together, play together, go everywhere together. ## "What makes us successful" (carried over, to keep building)

Blog success pulls toward utility + skimmability + trust (honest stats line, chill time surfaced early, sensory doneness cues, "looks wrong but isn't" reassurance, substitution answers before they improvise, a clean print-friendly card, one recipe = one clear success). Podcast success pulls toward personality + intimacy + slowness. Strategic answer: **be reliable enough that they trust the recipe, and characterful enough that they'd follow you even if they didn't need it.** Character work is the growth engine, not decoration.

### Writing style & division of labor (for our workflow)
- Stacey **over-delivers, then needs compression.** Strength is abundance — five great details where one would do. Perfect for brainstorm + first draft (give everything; cut later). Liability only at final polish ("earn the length"). So: lean into the abundance early; Muse tightens late. Don't self-censor volume while generating — that's where the gold is.
- **Spoken-style runs** (commas, ellipses, "essentially," "kind of," "honestly") read beautifully aloud (asset for podcast cadence) but soften on the page; for written work, occasionally swap a comma-run for a period to give a line punch.
- Bottom line: not "not a writer" — a **storyteller with an editor-shaped gap.** Voice and heart are hers; Muse's job is compression and rhythm.
