# Sunday Brunch With Giselle: Unified Multi-Stage Plan

## Purpose

This document converts the competitive review, two BMAD Party Mode rounds, existing project documentation, and the creator's clarified product decisions into one gated plan for building Sunday Brunch With Giselle into a top-quality recipe publication, weekly podcast, character world, and eventually a moderated community and merchandise brand.

This is a pre-launch plan. The current site is a private work in progress, and its placeholder recipes, photographs, reviews, ratings, and community states are valid design scaffolding. They become a concern only if they remain unlabeled or are accidentally released as genuine public content.

## Sources Consolidated

The plan incorporates:

- The 2026-06-11 competitive review of Serious Eats, Sally's Baking Addiction, Smitten Kitchen, Pinch of Yum, Damn Delicious, RecipeTin Eats, and A Spicy Perspective
- The first BMAD round with Sally, John, Winston, and Sophia
- The second consensus round covering UX, product management, architecture, podcast editorial strategy, community, and merchandise
- The existing PRD, brand guide, character personas, Episode 1 materials, and audio-production template
- The creator's settled decisions about Giselle, Phaedra, Tiana, Havok, the visual system, the weekly podcast, and future merchandise

## Settled Product Decisions

These decisions are not open optimization questions:

1. Sunday Brunch With Giselle is a memorial love letter to Giselle and a celebration of life, love, pets, baking, warmth, friendship, humor, whimsy, and magic.
2. The watercolor canvases, grain, floating shapes, cursor paw trails, Sheltie crossings, sparkles, glassmorphism, and decorative animation are protected product identity.
3. Performance improvements must come from engineering discipline, asset optimization, caching, compression, code organization, and progressive loading, not from stripping away the site's heart.
4. Giselle is the primary mascot, memorial heart, performed character, and AI-voiced weekly podcast co-host.
5. Phaedra, Tiana, and Havok are supporting mascots with established personalities, voices, relationships, boundaries, specialties, and recurring segments.
6. Stacey remains the human host, storyteller, recipe authority, editor, and final approver of character behavior and published dialogue.
7. The weekly podcast and recipe publication are the core product. Community and merchandise expand that core only after it is dependable and sustainable.
8. Fake reviews will not be used at launch. Prototype reviews and ratings must be removed, disabled, or clearly labeled before public release.
9. Real recipes, real photography, and authentic audience participation will replace current scaffolding.
10. Phase advancement is controlled by evidence and readiness gates, not by arbitrary dates.

## Strategic Position

### Recommended Market Position

> The most comforting, character-rich baking destination that is also one of the easiest recipe sites to cook from.

The site already owns the harder-to-copy half: emotional specificity, a recognizable world, character continuity, and a genuine reason to exist. The plan must now build the proof, cooking reliability, publishing discipline, and operational capacity that turn affection into trust.

### Launch Promise

> Sunday Brunch With Giselle helps pet-loving home bakers make something comforting and dependable while sharing a warm, whimsical Sunday with Stacey, Giselle, and the Sheltie family.

### Core User Jobs

1. Help me choose something worth baking.
2. Help me understand whether I can successfully make it.
3. Help me complete the recipe without confusion.
4. Keep me company while I bake.
5. Help me recover when something goes wrong.
6. Give me a reason to return next Sunday.
7. Let me participate in a warm pet-loving community when that community is ready.

## Strengths To Protect

The plan must preserve and extend these existing advantages:

- Immediately recognizable watercolor, scrapbook, Sheltie, and Sunday-brunch identity
- Clear emotional proposition and non-corporate point of view
- Strong desktop homepage narrative
- Recipe, podcast, and character integration
- Intent-based collections such as beginner-friendly, gifting, seasonal, and under 30 minutes
- Recipe scaling and US/metric conversion
- First-class allergen and dietary communication
- Print and copy concepts
- Character-delivered practical guidance
- Newsletter placement within the content journey
- Authentication, profile, analytics, and ratings foundations that can support later retention
- Meaningful accessibility intent already present in code and tests
- A calmer commercial environment than many ad-heavy recipe publishers
- A character ensemble and weekly audio format no reviewed competitor currently combines in the same way

## Competitive Lessons To Adopt

| Source pattern | Lesson for Sunday Brunch With Giselle |
|---|---|
| Serious Eats | Make testing, method, expertise, dates, and evidence visible. |
| Sally's Baking Addiction | Provide detailed baking guidance, troubleshooting, notes, and confidence-building proof. |
| Smitten Kitchen | Support intimate editorial voice and multiple paths for browsing by season, ingredient, meal, and serendipity. |
| Pinch of Yum | Make search immediate and category browsing approachable. |
| Damn Delicious | Keep practical decisions and recipe outcomes easy to scan. |
| RecipeTin Eats | Combine strong personality with excellent mobile cooking utility and cook-mode support. |
| A Spicy Perspective | Support broad, family-friendly categories and occasion discovery without losing authorship. |

The goal is not to copy their visual systems. It is to meet or exceed their recipe confidence and task reliability while retaining Sunday Brunch With Giselle's distinct world.

## Portfolio Rules

1. Utility earns trust; character earns preference.
2. Standard task labels come before branded delight.
3. Mobile cooking is the primary recipe experience.
4. Recipe reliability outranks feature count.
5. Real proof outranks simulated abundance.
6. The weekly podcast outranks speculative community infrastructure.
7. Character consistency is a product requirement.
8. Publishing cadence must fit actual operating capacity.
9. Accounts must provide immediate, explainable value.
10. Community opens only when moderation can be sustained.
11. Merchandise follows demonstrated affection and demand.
12. Accessibility and performance are implementation requirements, not arguments for flattening the art direction.

# Phase 0: Product Contract And Governance

## Objective

Turn the current vision and existing character work into an operating system for content, software, audio, and later commerce.

## Product And Editorial Deliverables

- Approve the launch promise and market position in this document.
- Consolidate the existing persona files into a maintained character canon.
- Define the primary audience-facing content pillars:
  - The Sunday Table
  - Recipes From Giselle's Kitchen
  - Tales From the Kitchen
  - The Sunday Brunch Podcast
  - Friends At Our Table
  - The Gentle Kitchen
- Define the production streams:
  - Cook With Stacey and Giselle
  - Sunday Brunch Podcast
  - Notes From the Shelties
  - Stories From Our Table
  - Friends At Our Table
- Create a production recipe quality checklist.
- Create a standard episode brief and release checklist.
- Create a correction and revision policy for recipes and episodes.
- Approve audience-facing AI-performance disclosure language.
- Define which subjects require Stacey's human voice rather than character treatment.

## Canonical Content Model

Define one authoritative model for:

- Recipes
- Ingredient groups
- Instructions and instruction sections
- Equipment
- Timing and yield
- Scaling and conversion eligibility
- Allergens and dietary disclosures
- Sensory checkpoints
- Substitutions and tested impacts
- Troubleshooting and failure recovery
- Storage, freezing, reheating, transport, and gifting
- Recipe testing and revision history
- Hero, process, and social media
- Podcast episodes
- Transcripts and chapters
- Characters and character-profile versions
- Collections
- Authors, testers, and reviewers
- Newsletter and campaign relationships
- Future contributor, pet, submission, consent, and merchandise records

## AI Voice Governance

- Maintain a voice-asset register for every generated performance.
- Record the provider, model, license, approved voice specification, settings, script version, generation date, editor, approver, and published use.
- Store source generation, edited dialogue, mastered episode, transcript, and release copy separately.
- Require human listening and approval before publication.
- Prohibit unsupervised character publication.
- Prevent AI output from becoming canon automatically.
- Create an incident and correction process for unsafe, inaccurate, inappropriate, or out-of-character audio.
- Audit existing voice-production documents for current provider terms, licensing, metadata, and rights before relying on them.
- Use original or properly licensed voices and obtain professional legal review where voice likeness, trademarks, publicity rights, or commercial merchandise may be implicated.

## Technical And Operational Deliverables

- Select the canonical content source: version-controlled structured content or a small headless CMS.
- Define stable IDs and relationships among recipes, episodes, characters, collections, and media.
- Define draft, review, scheduled, published, corrected, archived, and withdrawn states.
- Assign ownership for recipe testing, photography, editing, audio production, accessibility review, metadata, publication, correction, backup, and moderation.
- Define backup and restoration procedures for content, source audio, mastered audio, photographs, art, and configuration.
- Establish least-privilege administrative access and multifactor authentication.

## Explicit Exclusions

- Public submissions
- Public reviews
- Open comments
- Contests
- Merchandise checkout
- Large account or personalization systems
- Direct messages or social feeds

## Phase Gate

Phase 0 is complete when:

- A new recipe can be created without inventing fields or quality rules.
- A new episode can move from brief to archive through a documented workflow.
- Every Sheltie has approved canon, boundaries, specialties, and versioned reference material.
- AI voice disclosure, licensing review, approval, correction, and archival procedures exist.
- Ownership is assigned for every launch-critical task.

# Phase 1: Reliable Foundation

## Objective

Make the current website dependable, mobile-ready, accessible, internally coherent, and honest before adding more platform complexity.

## Critical Website Repairs

### Mobile Recipe Experience

- Repair the narrow, compressed recipe layout.
- Make the recipe title the primary `h1`.
- Keep ingredients, instructions, warnings, conversion controls, and primary actions readable without zooming.
- Establish large touch targets and stable control placement.
- Add a minimum active-cooking mode with:
  - Checkable ingredients
  - Step position memory
  - Previous and next controls
  - Screen wake toggle where supported
  - Print, copy, scale, and unit conversion
- Verify that decorative layers cannot intercept taps, keyboard focus, or screen-reader order.

### Discovery And Navigation

- Route one canonical recipe index and remove or retire the competing implementation.
- Read and apply collection query parameters.
- Persist filters and sorting in shareable URLs.
- Add result count, active-filter summary, clear-all action, and useful empty states.
- Use the existing mobile filter-drawer concept or an equivalent compact interaction.
- Improve desktop use of space with a responsive recipe grid.
- Add decision-critical card metadata:
  - Total time
  - Yield
  - Difficulty
  - Concise description
  - Occasion or season
  - Rating only when authentic data exists
- Add global search access.
- Create a real episode index instead of routing "Episodes" to a single episode.
- Expose About, Newsletter, accessibility, contact, and correction paths appropriately.

### Broken Or Misleading Actions

- Repair or temporarily remove the `/guestbook` CTA.
- Correct ingredient copying for structured ingredients.
- Pass canonical food imagery into recipe detail pages.
- Remove or disable nonpersistent public review interactions until moderation and storage exist.
- Clarify or reduce login prominence until saved content or another concrete account benefit exists.
- Ensure every public action either works or is clearly marked as upcoming.

### Page Hierarchy And Content Presentation

- Replace the generic "Recipe" heading with the actual recipe title.
- Replace or redefine the vague "Fresh" pill with meaningful publication, update, or testing status.
- Stop expanding the entire recipe inline on the homepage; link to the canonical detail URL.
- Shorten the mobile homepage path to recipes, episodes, and subscription without deleting the decorative system.
- Keep script typography for expressive accents rather than essential small controls.
- Strengthen delicate pastel contrast where required for WCAG 2.2 AA.
- Complete the footer with navigation, contact, legal, privacy, accessibility, correction, social, and newsletter links.
- Correct stale year and metadata.

## Engineering Coherence

- Consolidate recipe data, schema generation, and route ownership.
- Remove or complete dead recipe-template states and imports.
- Resolve the 11 known Vitest failures.
- Add browser tests for:
  - Homepage to collection
  - Search and filters
  - Recipe detail
  - Copy, print, scale, and conversion
  - Cook mode
  - Episode playback and transcript fallback
  - Newsletter signup
- Correct sitemap entries and generate them from canonical content.
- Generate valid Recipe, PodcastEpisode, BreadcrumbList, Organization, and relevant Person structured data.
- Scope broad CSS selectors and remove duplicated ownership that creates regressions.
- Add route-level error boundaries and useful loading, empty, and failure states.
- Establish CI gates for build, tests, accessibility checks, and critical browser journeys.

## Performance Without Removing The Decorative System

- Reserve dimensions for images, canvases, and decorative media to prevent layout shift.
- Use responsive image sizes and modern formats.
- Lazy-load route-specific and below-the-fold visual assets.
- Split route code and defer optional systems.
- Subset and preload fonts deliberately.
- Improve caching and compression.
- Preserve composition under reduced-motion preferences while changing motion behavior where required.
- Measure LCP, INP, and CLS as engineering feedback.

## Minimum Accessibility Standard

- WCAG 2.2 AA target
- Logical headings and landmarks
- Skip links
- Keyboard-complete navigation
- Visible focus
- Screen-reader-friendly recipe order
- Labels for controls and audio
- Adequate touch targets
- Zoom support
- Text equivalents for character audio
- Reduced-motion behavior that preserves the site's world
- Print-friendly recipes

## Explicit Exclusions

- Public profiles
- Persistent ratings and comments
- Personalized recommendations
- Open uploads
- Contests
- Merchandise
- Custom commerce

## Phase Gate

No public launch work advances until these journeys pass on representative mobile and desktop devices:

`arrive -> search or browse -> apply filters -> open recipe -> understand commitment -> cook -> print/copy/scale -> recover from an error -> find related episode -> listen or read transcript -> subscribe`

Required evidence:

- Production build passes.
- Unit and critical browser tests pass.
- Keyboard and screen-reader checks pass.
- Broken routes and actions are eliminated.
- Content and media can be restored from backup.
- No placeholder is capable of being mistaken for real public proof.

# Phase 2: Launch Library And Podcast Pilot

## Objective

Replace scaffolding with a small, trustworthy library and prove that a weekly character-led podcast can be produced sustainably.

## Recipe Library

Launch target:

- Minimum: 12 complete recipes
- Preferred initial library: 16 recipes
- Upper target before launch: 20 recipes

The library should demonstrate:

- Breakfast and brunch
- Baking and dessert
- Comfort food
- Seasonal recipes
- Beginner-friendly recipes
- At least one gift or hosting use case
- At least one recipe suitable for an eventual audio bake-along

Every launch recipe requires:

- Original finished-dish photography
- Process photography where uncertainty is high
- Clear title and outcome description
- Prep, cook, total time, yield, difficulty, and equipment
- Structured ingredient groups
- Tested scaling and conversion rules
- Detailed instructions
- Sensory checkpoints
- Common mistakes and recovery
- Tested substitutions and their likely effects
- Storage, freezing, reheating, and make-ahead guidance
- Allergen and dietary disclosures
- Pet-safe kitchen note where directly relevant
- "Why this works" or equivalent testing evidence
- Test date, tester, revision state, and update history
- Print, copy, scale, and mobile verification
- Search, collection, and episode relationships

## Podcast Pilot

Before launch:

- Complete four to six polished episodes.
- Hold at least two additional completed episodes in reserve.
- Complete four consecutive private weekly production cycles.
- Verify the full workflow:
  1. Episode brief
  2. Recipe test and editorial plan
  3. Script
  4. Character and safety review
  5. AI voice generation
  6. Human performance review
  7. Recording and editing
  8. Mastering
  9. Transcript correction
  10. Chapters and show notes
  11. Recipe and website links
  12. Accessibility review
  13. Metadata and artwork
  14. Scheduled release
  15. Archive and backup

Use a dedicated podcast host for durable RSS distribution and platform compatibility. The website should consume shared episode records or the feed rather than becoming the only audio host.

Every episode page requires:

- Title, description, date, artwork, duration, and cast
- Accessible player
- Podcast-app and download fallback
- Corrected HTML transcript
- Chapters
- Show notes
- Featured recipe and related content
- Character credits
- AI-performance disclosure
- One clear next action

## Editorial Streams

Launch around:

1. Cook With Stacey and Giselle
2. Sunday Brunch Podcast
3. Notes From the Shelties
4. Stories From Our Table
5. The Gentle Kitchen

"Friends At Our Table" remains visible as a future direction but does not solicit open community content until Phase 5.

## Character Use

- Giselle and Stacey lead the homepage and weekly episode.
- Phaedra owns food science and confidence-building explanations.
- Tiana owns sensory description, joy, and emotional reaction to food.
- Havok owns kitchen-chaos comedy, mishap framing, and occasional recovery lessons.
- Character modules must be useful, recognizable, and optional where a visitor wants only the recipe.
- Character dialogue never interrupts measurements, safety warnings, timers, or essential instructions.
- Each appearance follows the approved canon version.

## Newsletter And Retention Setup

- Create a welcome sequence explaining recipes, the Sunday letter, podcast releases, and character world.
- Connect recipe and episode pages to relevant subscription promises.
- Avoid placing conversion prompts directly beside vulnerable memorial passages.
- Track landing-page source, successful subscription, and later return behavior.

## Phase Gate

Phase 2 is complete when:

- The real recipe library meets its quality checklist.
- Four consecutive weekly pilot cycles finish on schedule.
- At least two completed episodes remain in reserve.
- Weekly production fits the creator's available time without reducing recipe quality.
- Every episode has a corrected transcript and related recipe or article.
- The characters remain recognizable in blind script readings.
- No release depends on emergency engineering work.

# Phase 3: Public Launch

## Objective

Release a focused, trustworthy recipe and podcast publication that clearly demonstrates both halves of the promise: warmth and reliability.

## Launch Criteria

### Content

- 12 to 20 complete recipes
- Four or more published or scheduled episodes
- Two completed reserve episodes
- Original recipe photography
- No fake reviews, ratings, testimonials, or dates
- Placeholder audit completed
- Character and episode canon reviewed

### Product

- Mobile recipe journeys pass
- Search, filters, sorting, counts, and URLs work
- Recipe and episode indexes work
- Copy, print, scale, conversion, and basic cook mode work
- Newsletter signup works
- Audio fallback and transcripts work
- All public actions are real or clearly marked as upcoming

### Trust And Safety

- About, contact, privacy, terms, accessibility, correction, and disclosure pages are available
- AI character-performance disclosure is clear
- Recipe corrections and update history have an owner
- Analytics collect only what is needed
- Secrets and administrative access are secured

### Operations

- Backups and restoration are tested
- Error monitoring and uptime checks are active
- Publishing checklists are usable by the creator
- Release rollback and correction procedures exist
- Metadata, sitemaps, structured data, and social previews validate

## Initial Launch Outcomes

- Visitors understand the concept within the first visit.
- Visitors can find a recipe appropriate to their needs.
- Bakers can complete a recipe successfully on mobile.
- Listeners understand the host and character relationship within one episode.
- Visitors move between recipes and episodes.
- The newsletter provides a credible reason to return.

# Phase 4: Retention And Cooking Companionship

## Objective

Move from a polished publication to a recurring Sunday ritual and a differentiated in-kitchen companion.

Run the measurement portion of this phase for at least eight to twelve weeks before using community or commerce to manufacture engagement.

## Product Features

- Full Cook Mode:
  - One step at a time
  - Persistent ingredient and step completion
  - Multiple timers
  - Screen wake
  - Large controls
  - High-contrast option
  - Screen-reader-optimized flow
- Confidence-adaptive instructions:
  - Guide me closely
  - Just the steps
- Sensory checkpoints
- Failure diagnosis
- Smart mise en place
- Tested substitution impact
- Gift and hosting mode
- Sunday plans with recipe, episode, shopping list, prep schedule, and serving moment
- Saved Sunday plans and continue-where-I-stopped behavior
- Optional audio bake-along
- Recipe-step audio markers
- Searchable character and episode archives

## Saves And Accounts

- Begin with local saved recipes if cross-device identity is not yet needed.
- Introduce accounts only when they provide:
  - Cross-device saves
  - Personal notes or plans
  - Submission management
  - Profile controls
  - Contest participation
  - Notification preferences
- Before accounts, define privacy, deletion, support, migration, and recovery.

## Search Improvements

- Evaluate real query logs.
- Add synonyms and typo tolerance based on observed needs.
- Search recipes, ingredients, collections, episodes, transcripts, and character specialties.
- Use failed searches to shape new recipes and collections.

## Character-Led Weekly Ritual

- Giselle and Stacey lead the weekly package.
- Supporting characters preview bounded specialties.
- Recipe pages show episode connections without forcing playback.
- Podcast pages link to exact recipe sections.
- Character audio remains optional, pausable, replayable, and transcribed.

## Metrics

- Weekly publishing reliability above 90 percent
- Returning visitor trend
- Newsletter conversion by page type
- Recipe-to-episode and episode-to-recipe movement
- Search success and no-result rate
- Print, copy, scale, save, and Cook Mode usage
- Recipe completion feedback
- Listener completion and repeat-listening indicators
- Recipe correction rate
- Common failure and substitution questions
- Qualitative evidence that people remember both Giselle and successful baking

## Phase Gate

Advance when:

- People return without contests or merchandise.
- Weekly publishing is sustainable.
- The episode reserve remains healthy.
- The site demonstrates meaningful movement between recipes, episodes, and newsletter.
- Cooking-support features reduce repeated confusion or failure.

# Phase 5: Moderated Community

## Objective

Invite pet-loving bakers into the world through controlled, consent-based editorial participation rather than an unmanaged social network.

## Controlled Build Order

1. Pet-story and recipe-request forms
2. Private moderation queue
3. Consent and publication records
4. Secure photograph uploads
5. Bake-result submissions
6. Pet and contributor profiles
7. Featured owner-and-pet stories
8. Controlled audience questions
9. Podcast cameo pilot

## Required Submission States

- Draft
- Submitted
- Under review
- Changes requested
- Approved
- Scheduled
- Published
- Rejected
- Withdrawn
- Archived

## Privacy, Consent, And Security

- Collect only necessary information.
- Validate upload type and size.
- Scan uploads for malware.
- Remove unnecessary image metadata.
- Define age restrictions and guardian consent.
- Record permission for website, podcast, social, newsletter, and commercial use separately.
- Provide reporting, correction, withdrawal, and takedown paths.
- Rate-limit and protect forms from spam.
- Use role-based moderation and audit logs.
- Define storage, retention, deletion, and backup rules.

## Editorial Model

- Feature selected submissions rather than opening a general feed.
- Giselle may welcome and celebrate contributors.
- Each supporting mascot may host a bounded category aligned with its specialty.
- Stacey handles grief disclosures, safety, consent, and sensitive responses.
- Character replies are reviewed before publication.
- Community advice is not presented as medical, veterinary, legal, or individualized professional advice.

## Explicit Exclusions

- Direct messaging
- Unmoderated comments
- Follower systems
- Open posting
- Public popularity rankings
- Real-time feeds
- Public voting
- User-run contests

## Operating Capacity

Define:

- Maximum submissions reviewed per week
- Maximum features produced per month
- Expected response time
- Named moderation owner
- Escalation process
- Handling of distressing, copyrighted, unsafe, or inappropriate material

## Phase Gate

Community participation must remain manageable for three consecutive months without disrupting recipes, accessibility, or the weekly podcast.

# Phase 6: Participatory Programming

## Objective

Turn successful moderated participation into distinctive recurring programming.

## Features

- Listener-requested recipes
- Featured pet cameos
- Owner, pet, and completed-bake spotlights
- Audience questions answered in character
- Seasonal ensemble episodes
- Community-requested Sunday plans
- Limited contests with official rules
- Judged challenges rather than uncontrolled popularity contests
- Community editorial calendar

## Operational Requirements

- Separate consent for audio performance, editing, distribution, promotion, and commercial reuse
- Release agreements for podcast cameos
- Clear contest eligibility, judging, prizes, fulfillment, and dispute handling
- Alternative participation routes for people who do not wish to appear publicly
- Accessibility support for submissions and participation

## Phase Gate

Advance only when consent, judging, prizes, fulfillment, moderation, and editorial quality can be handled consistently. Participation quality matters more than submission volume.

# Phase 7: Merchandise And Expanded Media

## Objective

Allow audience attachment to the Shelties, recurring sayings, artwork, recipes, and podcast moments to become tangible artifacts without turning the memorial into sales pressure.

## Demand Validation

Track:

- Frequently quoted sayings
- Favorite character exchanges
- High-engagement character artwork
- Requested products
- Repeat audience attachment
- Recipe-specific jokes and moments
- Seasonal illustrations

## Product Sequence

1. Stickers and small paper goods
2. Recipe cards, prints, and journals
3. One or two print-on-demand shirts
4. Mugs and kitchen textiles
5. Limited seasonal or episode-linked collections

## Technical And Commercial Approach

- Maintain print-resolution masters and provenance.
- Link products to approved character, artwork, saying, and canon versions.
- Complete trademark, copyright, licensing, and commercial-rights review.
- Use an external commerce provider for payments, tax, fulfillment, refunds, and order security.
- Keep payment data outside the website where possible.
- Integrate products through curated placements, not inside critical recipe instructions or vulnerable memorial passages.
- Test physical quality before sale.
- Define returns, customer service, margins, and fulfillment capacity.

## Memorial And Community Boundaries

- Do not use grief to pressure a purchase.
- Do not imply that purchasing proves love or loyalty.
- Do not say Giselle would want someone to buy a product.
- Do not use her death as sentimental product copy.
- Celebrate the characters, companionship, humor, and shared world.
- Obtain separate commercial permission before using any community pet or contributor.
- Make charitable claims specific, verifiable, and transparent.

## Phase Gate

Proceed when:

- Audiences demonstrate sustained attachment and demand.
- Artwork and saying rights are documented.
- Visual standards are stable.
- Samples meet quality expectations.
- Margins and fulfillment are viable.
- Commerce will not disrupt recipes or podcast production.
