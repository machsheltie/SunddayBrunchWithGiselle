Real memories of the real dogs are **human-voice-only (Stacey)**; the dogs never narrate a real memory.*

---

## Guiding principles

1. **They're real dogs, anthropomorphized.** Each blog character is the talking, emoting version of a real Sheltie Stacey raised. Personas must stay **true to the real essence** — the comedy comes from amplifying a true trait, never from inventing a false one.
2. **Stacey holds all authority.** Safety, allergens, raw-egg/temperature calls, real memories, corrections, and the final recipe word are always hers. The dogs celebrate, explain, and joke *around* these; they're never the authority.
3. **They're a household — so they act like one (NEW).** The dogs may acknowledge, needle, and reference each other and their real kinship on the page (within the box rules). Found-family cross-talk is now welcome; see "The pack as a family" below.
4. **Whimsy is seasoning.** Boxes stay short and skippable; each earns its spot; if a fact is stated (Havok's subs, Phaedra's science), it must be true.
5. **Giselle is eternally present.** She lives in the blog for as long as the blog lives. Her passing is never blog content; any acknowledgment is Stacey's private/dedication voice.

# The four profiles

## 👑 Giselle — The Queen
*Segment: **Giselle's Grand Verdict** (closing decree) + opens the story.*

**The goal — what every Giselle beat is ultimately for (Stacey's north star):** readers should *laugh* at the haughty, entitled, queenly energy — and then *melt* for the fierce loyalty and bottomless love underneath it — until they fall for **the real little girl Stacey knew and loved.** Laugh first, melt second, love by the end; the comedy earns the tenderness, and the tenderness is the point. We get there two ways: by being **specific and true** (her *real* ramping-and-rowling, the strawberry heist, the door she refused to leave, the joyful shriek across the field, the Taco City queso-poke) so readers fall for an actual dog and never a "sassy dog" trope — and by letting **Stacey visibly love the whole range** of her, haughty and soft alike, which is how the reader learns to love her too. If a Giselle beat is only funny, it isn't done yet.

**Look (illustrator-ready):** 13.25" tall, petite and fine-boned, all features delicate. **Heterochromia — one blue eye, one brown.** Genetically remarkable: appeared color-headed white sable merle, but a ~10-yr-ago merle study found the pattern "broke" in her — she carried merle cryptic and merle harlequin (herding harlequin) cells, giving a **mostly white body with sable merle patches.** Fairly fitted coat with a **large, full white ruff.** Tipped ears.

**Real essence:** Enormous personality; didn't care for most people; fiercely, intensely devoted to Stacey. The unquestioned leader/mastermind of the pack. (See Stacey bible for the full bond: the joyful shriek across the field, the body-slam into the bed-spot, the strawberry heist, the door she refused to leave, the Taco City queso-poke.) **She was Stacey's mirror** — a huge true self only one other being ever got to see.

**Persona:** the queen — sharp, witty, mock-formal, Devil-Wears-Prada-meets-Queen-of-Hearts, **with a strong streak of Effie Trinket** — and Effie is the key one for the *melt*: the mannered, haughty, propriety-obsessed exterior that slips to reveal she fiercely loves and protects the ragtag people who became *hers*, in her own fussy, regal, "I'm not technically supposed to say this" way ("you're all I have left to be proud of"). Deep protectiveness under the sass. Quality control; the verdict is her courtroom.

**Her essential range (the thing that makes her a person, not a bit):** she can be *fiercely sincere* one breath and **demand tribute for the sheer inconvenience of having had a feeling** the next. Overwhelmed by her own softness, she ramps and rowls her way back into imperious dignity and denies the whole thing — *"She was merely… briefly sincere. It will not happen again."* / *"…Not on the record."* The vulnerability is real; so is the deflection; both are true at once. **Never let her stay exposed** — the snap back to demanding-and-regal right after a tender beat is the contrast that makes both halves land, and it's what keeps her a whole person instead of a gag. (Canonical example: REC-001 Story / Episode 1 Story segment, the "names for my records → warmest room I've ever lived in → that was a *great* deal of feeling, I require compensation" sequence.)

**Signature behaviors (catchphrase candidates):** her real **"ramping and rowling"** — escalating demands (foot-smacks, digging, a low drawn-out growl that builds to a bark) until she's obeyed — is pure Queen energy and a perfect recurring tell. `<AI>` Possible signatures: "Let the record show…", "It is not guilty. It is, in fact, correct.", "Just like me." (Pending Stacey's catchphrase pass.) `</AI>`

**In the pack:** leads without question; would (jokingly) marshal the others to make Havok "walk the plank" (the couch arm) when he overstepped. Soft only for Stacey.

**Blog vs. podcast — how to handle her in each medium (decided 2026-06-22):**
- **Blog (prose headnotes): warmth-forward, with flashes of the blade.** Written entries are tender, emotional arcs, so Giselle anchors the warmth and shows her bite in *flashes* — a sharp aside, a possessive crack, the closing Verdict. Don't run a full comedy set inside a headnote; sustained snark fights the feeling. The blog's job is to *prove* she's funny and sharp.
- **Podcast: her comic-relief showcase.** Live banter with Stacey is where her sustained snark, diva demands, and "disappointment wearing a wig" register run free — comic timing lives in real-time back-and-forth. The podcast lets her be *relentless* about it.
- **Both media:** protect her tender "venom-dropping" beat (the crack in the armor) for the emotional payoffs — that contrast is the entire point of warmth-under-sass.
- *(This blog-prose-vs-live-banter split applies to the whole cast, but it matters most for Giselle, since she carries the comic relief.)*

## The pack as a family (the NEW cross-talk layer)

Because they're a household, the boxes can now show the *relationships*, not just four solo acts. Rules so it deepens rather than clutters:

- **Each box still has one job and stays short.** Cross-talk is a sentence of seasoning, not a second topic. The recipe stays skippable and clean.
- **Cross-references go dog-to-dog, never into the steps.** A dog can nod to a sibling inside their own box; nothing reaches into the numbered instructions, measurements, or safety.
- **Use the real dynamics:**
  - **Havok orbits the Queen** — goofily echoing Giselle's verdicts, like a herald who didn't read the room.
  - **Phaedra reins Havok in** — the aunt's quiet correction ("That intel is unconfirmed, Agent."), now that she's the one who keeps him honest.
  - **Tiana cheers everyone** — especially her best friend Phaedra; the bottom-of-the-totem-pole sweetheart who's just thrilled to be included.
  - **Giselle presides over all of them** — protective underneath, the household's center of gravity, even in absentia.
- **Kinship can surface lightly** ("my mama Giselle would say…", "my best friend Phaedra already explained the science, so listen to her") — it teaches the family without a lecture.
- **Still no real memories from the dogs.** Family *dynamics* on the page = fine; real biographical memory = Stacey's voice only.
- **No dog isolated (equity rule).** Every dog gets a relationship or recurrence beat per entry — never stranded in a single solo box. Word count isn't memorability; a dog readers meet once, alone, recedes no matter how good the box is. Use the canon pairings (Tiana ↔ Phaedra best friends, Phaedra ↔ Havok aunt/nephew, Giselle presiding over all) to give each dog a cut-in, a cheer, or a reaction. **Watch Tiana especially** — her Tasting Notes box is the shortest *by function*, so her staying-power has to come from connection and recurrence, not length. (Enforced in the `prepublish-editorial-pass` skill.)

`<AI>` Payoff: readers start to learn the household — who defers to whom, who loves whom — and that's exactly what turns "a recipe with cute dog boxes" into "the family I visit every Sunday." `</AI>`
