# Issue: Texture-metaphor crowding (cloud / silk / satin / mousse) — and trait-synonym crowding generally

**Status:** OPEN (recurring — watch every dessert entry AND any bio/memorial built around one defining trait). **REC-001 instances: CLEARED at v8** ("cloud" now belongs to Tiana only; summary uses "silky"). **About-page memorials: OPEN (new instance, 2026-06-22)** — Athena's "supervisor" trait described via 5 overlapping synonyms (see below). The pattern stays open as a series-level watch-item.
**Severity:** Low–medium. Reader-facing when the same concept gets named several near-identical ways in a short span.
**Scope:** Recipe summaries, story prose, and the Tiana/Phaedra segments — anywhere the same dish's texture gets described more than once. First observed on REC-001 French Silk Pie. **Same mechanism now seen in bio/memorial prose** (`Personas/About-Page-Memorials-and-Dedication-DRAFT.md`): a character's one defining trait restated through a cluster of synonyms reads as a thin word bank, exactly like texture metaphors do.

## What it is

A silky, airy dessert invites a small pool of the same metaphors — cloud, silk, satin, mousse, velvet — and the page describes the texture in several places (summary, story, Tiana's tasting notes, Phaedra's science). Without coordination, two surfaces reach for the same image, so it reads like the only image available.

## Evidence (REC-001)

- **"cloud" collision (pre-v8):** the recipe one-liner said "cloud-soft" while Tiana, inches away, said "a little chocolate cloud that learned some manners." Resolved in v8 by switching the summary to "silky" and letting Tiana keep "cloud."
- **Adjacent synonyms in play:** "silky" (summary, Phaedra "fine and silk"), "satin-smooth" (Phaedra, whyItWorks), "mousse-like" (summary, Phaedra, whyItWorks). "silk" is the through-line (it's literally French *Silk* Pie), so repeating it is on-brand; the others should not double up with it or each other on the visible page.

## Evidence (About-page memorials, 2026-06-22) — trait-synonym crowding

- **Athena's "supervisor" trait, 5 overlapping terms in one short span:** "never met a job she didn't plan to **personally oversee**" → "**keeping an eye on** the whole operation" → "the only title that ever really stuck: the **supervisor**" → "what she was mostly **supervising**" → "**watching over** a much younger… me." The trait is her defining one, so some repetition is intentional, but five near-synonyms blunt the joke. Keep the title that lands ("the supervisor") + one supporting verb; vary or cut the rest.
- **Giselle's dominance trait** is also restated several ways ("supervised everything," "had opinions about all of it," "queen at the head of the table," "handing down her verdicts," "entirely in charge"). Less crowded than Athena's because the phrasings are more varied, but it's the same temptation — watch it in future memorials.

## Why it matters

When two voices use the same metaphor for one texture, the writing feels generated from a thin word bank, and each character's sensory description loses distinctiveness (Tiana's job is a *unique* sensory take; Phaedra's is mechanism).

## Direction for future entries

- Assign textures deliberately: let "silk" be the brand through-line; give Tiana ONE distinctive image (her cloud), and keep Phaedra's language mechanical (air bubbles, dissolving sugar) rather than poetic, so they don't compete.
- Pre-publish check: list every texture descriptor on the page; if the same image appears in two visible places for the same dish, change one.
- **For bios/memorials:** the same check applies to a subject's defining trait. List every word naming that trait; keep the one phrase that lands hardest plus at most one support, and cut or vary the rest so the trait reads as observed, not word-banked.
