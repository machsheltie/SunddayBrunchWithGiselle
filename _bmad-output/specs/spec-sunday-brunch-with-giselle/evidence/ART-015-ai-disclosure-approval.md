## Approved Wording

> The Shelties are authored characters performed with synthetic voices created using AI tools. Stacey writes, directs, edits, and approves every published performance. Giselle's character is inspired by the real Giselle and created in her honor; it is not presented as her literal consciousness.

## Required Placements

The disclosure must appear as accessible text:

- On character and podcast information pages.
- On every episode page.
- In durable show notes or feed metadata.
- Near any standalone character-audio player.
- In transcripts when context requires it.

The disclosure does not need to interrupt every scene or joke.

## Accessibility Treatment

- The wording must be available as text and cannot exist only in spoken audio, an image, a tooltip, or inaccessible metadata.
- Standalone audio controls must have a nearby programmatic text relationship to the disclosure.
- Show-note and feed copies must remain understandable when consumed outside the website.
- Material changes to the disclosure require a new version and Stacey's approval.

## Rights Basis

- ART-016 records paid ElevenLabs Voice Design, original synthetic voices, approved production models, controlled commercial uses, prohibited impersonation, human approval, and the provider training opt-out.
- The disclosure does not claim a human performer, a cloned person, or the real Giselle's consciousness.
- Provider terms must be rechecked before first public release and after any material provider, plan, model, voice, or intended-use change.

## Signoff

Stacey approved the exact wording, placements, accessibility treatment, and rights basis on 2026-06-11. ART-015 is accepted.
