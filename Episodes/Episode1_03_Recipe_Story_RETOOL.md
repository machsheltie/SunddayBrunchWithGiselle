# French Silk Pie — Recipe Story (RETOOL working draft)
*"The pie that started everything." Story segment. Cast: Stacey, Giselle (present-tense co-host);
Athena referenced in human-voice memory. Built against SCRIPT-VOICE-GUIDE.md + canonical REC-001 story.*

[Sound: the kitchen settles — the Echo Dot dropping to something slow, a mug set down, a dog turning a circle on a cushion and flopping with a sigh.]

**STACEY:** The first French Silk Pie I ever made was a bribe.

**GISELLE:** [a long-suffering sigh, from the head of the table — which is hers, as is everything else in here] …A *bribe.* Darling. You had me at home. You never needed leverage.

**STACEY:** [softly] You weren't the one I was trying to win over, Your Majesty.

**GISELLE:** A baffling allocation of effort. But continue.

**STACEY:** I was twenty-something, and I was dating someone whose family did Thanksgiving at full volume. The kind of people who laugh over each other and lob teasing across the room like it's a sport. I love a loud, warm kitchen *now.* Back then? It flattened me. I'd stand at the edge of the room forgetting my own punchlines — going quiet in exactly the moments I wanted to be charming.

So I gave up on talking my way into that family. And I decided to bake my way in instead. If I couldn't be the funny one, I'd be the one who showed up with something unforgettable.

**STACEY:** What followed was a week my dogs have still not forgiven. Five recipes lined up on the counter. Cookbooks splayed open. Sticky notes everywhere, in my own increasingly unhinged handwriting — *beat the eggs LONGER. More butter. WHY grainy.*

**GISELLE:** Five recipes. In my kitchen. For people who are not me. [a regal pause] I cannot fathom the motivation.

**STACEY:** You weren't there yet, Giselle. That was Athena's kitchen.

**GISELLE:** [a beat — uncharacteristically quiet] …Ah. Yes. Hers.

**STACEY:** [warm, to the listener] Athena. Giselle's great-grandmother. The one who started all of this — the dog who earned the title *the supervisor* before any of these four were even an idea. She was my heart dog. My constant shadow — the one who followed me room to room, sink to stove, who'd take up her post and *watch* me long before Giselle ever inherited the job. And she did not nap. She watched. Bright-eyed, almost unsettlingly human, like she was following every word and had notes on my technique. And under all that watching, she was hoping — a little shark in a thirteen-inch frame, up like a shot at the first clink of a bowl, completely certain this disaster would finally drop something good on the floor.

**STACEY:** Giselle holds that cushion now.

**GISELLE:** As is correct.

**STACEY:** Where Athena watched and hoped in silence — her great-granddaughter *narrates.* Out loud. Mid-step. Fully prepared to tell you she could do the whole thing better, faster, and with considerably more dignity.

**GISELLE:** The throne is hereditary. The standards came with it. [crisply] Mine simply arrive with commentary.

**STACEY:** The one thing I refused to agonize over, then or now? The crust. Freezer aisle. I have never once been sorry, and neither should you.

**STACEY:** And then — after a week of that — I landed on the version I still make. The one I'm about to give you. I tasted it and I said "oh" out loud, alone in my kitchen.

**GISELLE:** [aside] Athena heard "oh" and assumed it meant cheese.

**STACEY:** [a small laugh] She lifted her whole head off the cushion. Certain her moment had finally come. …It had not. But the pie? Glossy. Light. Rich. Structured, but silky. I carried it into that Thanksgiving like a holy relic. Sure — *so* sure — it would say everything I couldn't. *She's quiet, but look how much she cares.*

[Sound: the room tone thins, just slightly — the way a memory does when you walk all the way back into it.]

**STACEY:** Someone took a bite. Tilted their head. Gave me a polite little smile. And said… "it's… a bit rich."

[a beat]

**STACEY:** And friend — what I heard was, *you're a bit much.*

**GISELLE:** [low — dangerous — none of the performance] Names.

**STACEY:** Giselle.

**GISELLE:** I would simply like the names. For my records.

**STACEY:** [gentle] We're not naming names, sweet girl. It was twenty years ago. I've grown since then — I forgave it a long time ago. They weren't cruel. They just weren't mine.

[a beat]

**STACEY:** But I see you down there, ready to go to war over a *pie.* That's the part that actually fixes it. So thank you. I mean that.

**GISELLE:** [quieter; the armor all the way down] I do not like it when small people make you smaller.

**STACEY:** I know you don't.

**GISELLE:** You are the warmest room I have ever lived in. [a beat — gathering her dignity back up] I simply refuse to have it cold on your account. That is all.

**STACEY:** [a little undone] …Okay. Thank you, my love.

[a beat of quiet — and then it begins. A paw smacks down on Stacey's foot. Then the other. A dig at the floor, a low grumble winding up into that long, theatrical *rowl* — a Queen overwhelmed by her own sincerity, ramping back into herself.]

**GISELLE:** [up over the rowl, escalating] That was a *great* deal of feeling. I will require compensation. The good cushion. The *entire* cushion. And whatever is currently on that counter.

**STACEY:** [laughing, a little wet] — oh, there's the rowl. *There* she is. There's the Giselle I know and love.

**GISELLE:** [grandly] She was never gone. She was merely… briefly sincere. It will not happen again.

**STACEY:** It absolutely will.

**GISELLE:** [a beat] …Not on the record.

**STACEY:** [to the listener, still smiling] Here's the part I didn't understand until years later — and it's the reason this pie is still my favorite thing to make. They were *right* that it's rich. And completely *wrong* about what that meant. Some people taste all that abundance and want it dialed down. And some of us taste it and say… *more, please.*

This is a pie for the second kind of person.

**GISELLE:** [quietly, with none of her usual venom] The right people see thoughtfulness where others see extravagance. [a pause] Those are the only people worth baking for anyway.

**STACEY:** [softly] …Yeah. They are.

**STACEY:** That "too rich" pie became the one people now request for their birthdays. It became the slow Sunday afternoons. The stories. A whole houseful of opinionated dogs who insist on a say in every dessert I make. The bribe that quietly became my entire world.

So this is the one we start with. The first recipe — made for anyone who's ever been told they're too much by people who just wanted easy.

**GISELLE:** [grandly, recovered] Enough sentiment. There is a pie to supervise.

**STACEY:** [laughing] There she is. Okay. Let me show you how to say the thing you don't have the words for —

[Sound: a chair pushing back, the click of nails already heading for the kitchen.]
